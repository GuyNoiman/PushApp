**2 — The truth**
> Somewhere there is a version of you who kept the promise.
> Who quit. Who finished it. Who called more often. You didn’t fail because you didn’t care.
*A medium-to-large change in intentions produces only a small-to-medium change in behavior. — Sheeran & Webb, 2016*

**3 — The problem (reframe)**
> The gap is not between you and your dreams. It is between intention and action.
> People don’t lack goals. They lack a system that keeps them on the path when life pulls them off it.
*The intention–behavior gap. — Sheeran & Webb, 2016*

**4 — The villain**
> The most advanced technology ever built captures your attention — it never returns it to your life.
> And we try to change alone, at the moment we are most likely to quit.

**5 — The insight (the turn)**
> The tracker was never the problem. The moment you drift is.
> Every habit app helps you on your best day. Nobody built for the Tuesday you skip.
*Just-in-time adaptive interventions. — Nahum-Shani et al., 2018*

**8 — How it works**
> Commit → Drift → Intervene.
> 1) Commit to a Journey (a real path, not a checkbox). 2) Drift (everyone does). 3) PushApp intervenes — a nudge, a friend, or nothing at all.
*If-then plans raise attainment, d=0.65 across 94 tests. — Gollwitzer & Sheeran, 2006. Specific goals beat vague ones. — Locke & Latham.*

**9 — Human support (second act)**
> Most people will help someone they love. Almost no one knows how to ask.
> PushApp turns "I’m rooting for you" into something real, at the moment it matters.
*Supportive accountability improves adherence. — Mohr, Cuijpers & Lehman, 2011*

**10 — Magic moment**
> “I didn’t think I could stay consistent… but I actually did.”
*Missing a single day doesn’t derail habit formation; median 66 days, range 18–254. — Lally et al., 2010*

*Identity-based motivation: identity-congruent difficulty sustains action. — Oyserman*
