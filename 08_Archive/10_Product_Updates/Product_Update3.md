## Approved Decisions

### Buddy is the product's face

One of the most significant product decisions is redefining Buddy's role.

Buddy is **not** the user.

Buddy is **not** merely an avatar.

Buddy is the user's companion throughout their entire personal-growth journey.

The relationship should feel closer to:

- a companion
- a coach
- a supportive friend

than a virtual pet.

The user is growing.

Buddy is growing alongside them.

The user's achievements are reflected through Buddy rather than through a static profile picture.

---

### Buddy replaces the traditional profile picture

Instead of showing a real profile picture throughout the application, the preferred direction is that every user is represented by their Buddy.

Examples:

Friend list

Inbox

Journey participants

Leaderboards

Support requests

Ally cards

Chat

This creates a stronger emotional connection to Buddy while also reinforcing the game identity.

Buddy should visually communicate:

- current level
- cosmetic items
- rarity
- personality
- overall progression

without requiring users to open the profile.

---

### Buddy Customization

Buddy should become one of the strongest long-term retention systems.

Customization should include:

- outfits
- accessories
- hairstyles (where applicable)
- colors
- animations
- emotes
- rare cosmetics
- seasonal cosmetics

Future ideas:

different Buddy species

robotic companions

fantasy creatures

cute monsters

non-human AI companions

The intention is **not** to imitate Pokémon or Sims directly, but to create an original visual language that naturally represents an AI companion.

---

### Emotional State

One idea that resonated strongly is allowing Buddy's emotional state to reflect the user's progress.

Important principle:

Buddy should never create guilt.

Buddy should never punish.

Buddy should never shame.

Instead Buddy should communicate emotions such as:

"I'm excited."

"I'm proud of us."

"I've missed working together."

"I'm glad you're back."

"We can always continue."

The emotional state exists to strengthen attachment rather than pressure.

---

### Buddy Growth

Buddy grows because both Buddy and the user are learning together.

This is an important philosophical distinction.

The user gains experience in real life.

Buddy gains experience because they shared that journey.

This creates the feeling that neither one progresses alone.

---

## Future Vision

### Buddy Conversations

Future versions of PushApp may allow users to have conversations directly with Buddy.

Buddy becomes the interface for AI.

Instead of opening "AI Chat", users simply talk to Buddy.

Possible conversation topics:

- planning
- motivation
- reflection
- roadmap creation
- reviewing difficult weeks
- adapting Journeys
- choosing the next Journey

This feature is explicitly considered **Future Vision** and should not be assumed to exist in the MVP.

---

### Adaptive Personality

Buddy should eventually adapt to the user's preferred communication style.

Examples:

supportive

challenging

friendly

playful

minimal

professional

The adaptation should happen gradually by learning from user behaviour rather than explicit configuration whenever possible.

---

### Adaptive Communication Timing

AI should learn:

- preferred notification times
- preferred wording
- notification frequency
- response likelihood
- periods of high availability

The goal is not to send more notifications.

The goal is to send fewer, better notifications.

This personalization is considered one of the future premium AI capabilities.

---

### Buddy-first AI

The user should rarely feel they are using AI.

Instead they should feel they are interacting with Buddy.

Buddy becomes the product identity.

AI becomes an implementation detail.

---

## Open Questions

### Should Buddy hatch?

One idea discussed was that users initially receive an egg rather than a complete Buddy.

As users progress, Buddy hatches.

Advantages:

- emotional attachment
- memorable onboarding
- surprise
- collectible system

Status:

Future idea.
Not yet approved.

---

### Dynamic Buddy in notifications

Question:

Should Buddy appear visually inside push notifications?

Current thinking:

Probably not.

Static notifications are technically simpler.

Buddy should become visually alive primarily inside the application.

Status:

Open.

---

### Voice

Should Buddy eventually speak?

Potential future capability.

Depends on AI maturity and cost.

Not part of MVP.

---

### Repository Update Notes

This entire Buddy direction is newer than the current repository documentation.

When merging into the repository:

- Replace any wording describing Buddy as a simple avatar.
- Treat Buddy as the emotional center of the product.
- Ensure every screen uses Buddy consistently as the user's public identity.
- Keep all AI-related capabilities clearly separated into **Future Vision** unless explicitly approved for the first commercial version.
