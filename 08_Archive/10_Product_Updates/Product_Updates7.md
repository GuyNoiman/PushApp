## Purpose

One of the most important outcomes of the recent design sessions was realizing that we must constantly distinguish between four different product stages.

Many conversations became confusing because ideas from different stages were mixed together.

Future documentation should therefore classify every feature according to its intended delivery stage.

---

# Product Stages

## Stage 0 — Vision

Purpose:

Describe the ultimate product.

This document should intentionally ignore implementation complexity.

Questions answered here:

What do we want PushApp to become in five years?

What emotional experience should users have?

What makes PushApp unique?

No technical limitations should influence this document.

---

## Stage 1 — Proof of Concept (POC)

Purpose:

Validate that users actually want the product.

The POC should answer only one question:

"If this existed today, would people actually use it?"

The POC is not expected to validate the entire product.

Instead it validates the core hypothesis.

Current philosophy:

Do not let the POC reduce the long-term vision.

The POC is only an experiment.

---

## Stage 2 — MVP

Purpose:

Deliver the smallest product capable of providing meaningful value.

Important clarification from recent discussions:

The MVP should **not** simply be the cheapest product to build.

It should already demonstrate why PushApp is fundamentally different.

Current thinking:

The MVP may already include premium AI functionality if that functionality is central to differentiation.

Examples:

Adaptive notifications.

Buddy communication style.

Journey recommendations.

Simple Journey generation.

---

## Stage 3 — First Commercial Version

Purpose:

Launch a product people are willing to pay for.

The first commercial version should already feel polished.

Examples of functionality likely expected by this stage:

Journey Templates

Marketplace foundations

Buddy customization

AI personalization

Achievements

Coins

XP

Friends

Support system

Journey completion celebrations

---

## Long-Term Vision

The following ideas intentionally belong here rather than earlier stages.

These ideas should remain documented even if they are years away.

Examples:

Buddy voice.

Buddy conversations.

Egg onboarding.

Interactive Journey Experiences.

Creator Marketplace.

Business Journeys.

Adaptive coaching.

AI-generated personal roadmaps.

Dynamic intervention engine.

Advanced psychological personalization.

# Product Complexity

One recurring discussion throughout the design sessions concerned product complexity.

Observation:

PushApp is becoming a very ambitious platform.

Examples:

Journey Engine

Buddy

Gamification

Marketplace

AI

Adaptive notifications

Community

Interactive experiences

At first this created concern.

However an important decision was made.

We should **never shrink the vision simply because implementation is difficult.**

Instead:

Keep the vision intact.

Deliver it gradually.

# Summary of the Design Sessions

The most important shift made during these sessions was changing the way PushApp is perceived.

PushApp is no longer viewed as:

- a habit tracker,
- a productivity tool,
- or a gamified task manager.

Instead, PushApp is now defined as:

> **A platform that helps people become who they choose to be.**

Everything else—the Journey Engine, Buddy, AI, gamification, community, marketplace and adaptive experiences—exists to support that single mission.

This principle should become the north star for every future product decision.
